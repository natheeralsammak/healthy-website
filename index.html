How to Stay Consistent & Actually Commit to Your Diet

The biggest challenge isn't starting — it's not stopping. Here are the proven strategies that work.

1

Start Ridiculously Small

Don't try to overhaul everything in week one. Pick one habit — drink one extra glass of water per day, or swap one meal. Small wins build the neural pathways that make healthy behaviour automatic.

2

Make It Identity-Based, Not Goal-Based

"I am someone who eats well" is far more powerful than "I want to lose 5kg." Goals have an end; identity doesn't. Ask yourself: what would a healthy person do right now?

3

Plan & Prep on Sundays

Decision fatigue is real. When food is already cooked and portioned, you'll make better choices 90% of the time. Spend 2 hours on Sunday cooking proteins, grains and chopping vegetables for the week.

4

Use the 80/20 Rule — Not Perfection

Eat on-plan 80% of the time and enjoy 20% flexibility. Rigid "all-or-nothing" thinking is the #1 reason diets fail. Missing one meal doesn't ruin a week — giving up does.

5

Track Progress, Not Just Weight

The scale lies. Use our Body Calculator to track body fat %, lean mass, and waist measurements weekly. Seeing real body composition change is far more motivating than a single number.

6

Anchor Habits to Existing Routines

"After my morning coffee, I will prepare my lunch." Habit stacking — linking a new behaviour to an existing one — dramatically increases follow-through rate. Your brain loves patterns.

7

Protect Your Sleep Like Your Most Important Meal

Sleep deprivation increases hunger hormones by 15–20%, tanks your motivation to exercise, and makes your brain crave high-calorie foods. 7–9 hours is not optional — it's the foundation.

8

Find Your "Why" and Write It Down

Do you want to have energy to play with your kids? Live to 90 without disease? Fit into clothes confidently? Write it down, photograph it, and read it when motivation dips. Emotion beats willpower.

9

Remove Friction, Add Convenience

Don't keep chips in the house. Do keep washed fruit at eye level. Put your gym bag by the door the night before. The easier healthy choices are, the more likely you are to make them without thinking.

10

Never Miss Twice

You will slip up. Everyone does. The golden rule: never miss the same healthy behaviour two days in a row. One miss is a blip. Two misses in a row starts becoming a new (bad) habit.

"We are what we repeatedly do. Excellence, then, is not an act but a habit."
