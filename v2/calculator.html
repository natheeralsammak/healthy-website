Body Fat % Estimation

Choose a method — Navy, BMI-based, or Skinfold (Jackson-Pollock)

The US Navy circumference method (Hodgdon & Beckett, 1984) uses body circumferences to estimate body fat. Error: ±3–4%.

Measure at the narrowest point (men) or at the navel (women)
Measure just below the larynx, sloping downward to the front
Measure at the widest point of the hips

The Deurenberg formula (1991) derives body fat % from BMI, age and gender. Error: ±5%. No measurements needed — uses height/weight from Step 1.

ℹ️ This method uses only your height, weight and age from Step 1. No additional measurements required.

Jackson & Pollock 3-site skinfold (1978/1980). Uses a skinfold calliper to measure subcutaneous fat at 3 sites. Error: ±3–4%.

Diagonal fold, mid-pectoral
Vertical fold, 2 cm right of navel
Vertical fold, front mid-thigh
Vertical fold, back of upper arm
Diagonal fold, above iliac crest
Vertical fold, front mid-thigh
